2. Overview of Relevant Sustainable Development Goals
Purpose: To show you understand the specific SDGs your project aligns with. Don't list all 17; only focus on the most relevant ones (typically 1-3 goals).
Suggested Content:
Briefly describe the SDGs that are most relevant to your project. For each one, include:

It is central to engineering, as it directly involves developing efficient systems, new technologies, and robust physical and digital infrastructure."
3. Primary SDG Alignment
Purpose: To clearly identify the single most relevant SDG your project supports and provide a detailed justification. This is your strongest link.
Suggested Content:
Clearly state the primary SDG your project addresses.
Provide a detailed justification with direct references to your project's features and objectives.
Template:
"This project primarily contributes to SDG [Number]: [SDG Name].
The project's focus on [Mention a key aspect of your project, e.g., developing an affordable water purification system, creating a renewable energy monitoring tool, building an accessible education platform] directly supports the targets of this goal, specifically [mention a specific target if possible, e.g., ensuring access to clean water, promoting sustainable infrastructure]. By [Explain how your project does this], it aligns with the core objective of [Reiterate the SDG's objective]."
Concrete Example (for a Water Quality Monitoring System):
"This project primarily contributes to SDG 6: Clean Water and Sanitation. The development of a low-cost, real-time water quality monitoring system directly supports Target 6.3: improving water quality by reducing pollution. The system enables communities to detect contaminants early, preventing waterborne diseases and promoting sustainable water management."
4. Secondary SDG Alignment
Purpose: To show the broader, often indirect, impact of your project. This demonstrates holistic thinking.
Suggested Content:
Identify one or two secondary SDGs that your project indirectly supports.
The connection can be through the technology used, the methodology, or a side-effect of the primary goal.
Template:
"Additionally, this project secondarily supports SDG [Number]: [SDG Name]. While not the primary focus, the project's use of [Mention a technology/method, e.g., solar power, open-source software, user-centered design] promotes [Mention the secondary SDG's objective, e.g., affordable and clean energy, reduced inequalities]. Furthermore, by [Explain the indirect effect, e.g., providing data for better health decisions, creating a tool that can be used in remote areas], it contributes to [Reiterate the secondary SDG]."
Concrete Example (Continuing from the water system):
"Additionally, this project secondarily supports SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being. By preventing the consumption of contaminated water, the system directly reduces the incidence of waterborne diseases, thereby improving public health. It also indirectly supports SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure by demonstrating an innovative, locally deployable sensing technology."
